Query: What are the optimal co-culture or three-dimensional organoid platforms incorporating HLA-DQ2/8-positive antigen-presenting cells, dendritic cells or monocyte-derived macrophages and gluten-specific T cells to study antigen processing, presentation dynamics and T-cell receptor activation in response to deamidated gliadin, and which functional readouts (e.g., cytokine secretion profiles, T-cell proliferation, activation markers) yield mechanistic insight?

Optimal platforms for studying antigen processing, presentation dynamics, and T-cell receptor (TCR) activation in response to deamidated gliadin combine physiologically relevant three‐dimensional (3D) human organoid systems with co-culture approaches that incorporate HLA-DQ2/8-positive antigen-presenting cells (APCs), such as dendritic cells (DCs) and monocyte-derived macrophages, together with gluten-specific T cells.

One highly effective system uses air-liquid interface (ALI) duodenal organoids derived directly from patient biopsies. These ALI organoids preserve the native intestinal architecture including the epithelium, mesenchyme, and a diverse repertoire of tissue-resident immune cells. Importantly, these organoids retain HLA-DQ2/8-positive APCs—such as dendritic cells and macrophage subsets—and gluten-specific T cells, which are intrinsic to the tissue. The inherent presence and preserved distribution of these immune cells allow the organoids to model antigen processing and presentation dynamics in a tissue context that closely approximates in vivo conditions (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 1-2, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 2-4).

Alternatively, 3D co-culture systems can be established by introducing HLA-DQ2/8-homozygous APCs (for example, Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)-transformed B cells or in vitro differentiated dendritic cells/monocyte-derived macrophages) into reconstituted organoid platforms or traditional co-culture setups with isolated gluten-specific CD4+ T cells. These setups are tailored to study antigen presentation—where deamidated gliadin peptides are loaded onto APCs—and the subsequent TCR activation in T cells. This hybrid strategy allows fine-tuning of cell composition and supplementation with critical cytokines like interleukin-2 (IL-2) and interleukin-7 (IL-7) that enhance maintenance and functionality of CD45+ immune cells within the system (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 4-6, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 8-9).

The optimal readouts in these systems yield mechanistic insights through multiple functional assays:

1. Cytokine Secretion Profiles:
   Cytokine quantification is central for monitoring the inflammatory and activation status within the co-culture. Readouts include detection of IL-7, IL-15, IL-2, IFN-γ, and a panel of chemokines (such as CCL3, CCL4, CCL5, CCL20) using multiplex assays like Luminex or ELISA. These cytokine measurements directly correlate immune cell activation and provide evidence of specific gliadin-induced responses (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 6-8, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 8-9).

2. T-cell Proliferation and Activation Markers:
   T-cell proliferation is frequently monitored via flow cytometric quantification of CD3+, CD4+, or CD8+ T cells, often using incorporation assays or proliferation dyes. Activation markers such as CD25, CD69, and the induction of cytotoxic receptors (for example, NKG2C and NKG2D on CD8+ T cells) are assessed to document antigen-specific TCR activation. Tetramer staining (using soluble HLA-DQ2.5-gliadin complexes) combined with TCR repertoire sequencing further validates the clonal expansion of gluten-specific T cells (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 6-8, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 9-11, johnson2024themultipleroles pages 34-36).

3. Apoptosis and Epithelial Damage Assays:
   Since epithelial damage is a hallmark of celiac disease, functional readouts include assays for epithelial apoptosis. These are typically performed using immunofluorescence staining for cleaved caspase-3 and fluorogenic caspase-3/7 activity assays, providing quantitative measures of gliadin-induced epithelial cell death (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 4-6, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 37-41).

4. High-resolution Transcriptional and TCR Clonotype Analysis:
   Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), coupled with TCR reconstruction (using platforms like 10X Genomics Chromium and analytical tools such as GLIPH), allows detailed transcriptomic profiling of immune cells. This enables the correlation of T-cell activation states with clonal expansion and the discovery of antigen-specific TCR motifs, adding another mechanistic layer to the analysis (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 16-18, wyrozemski2023transcriptomicandfunctional pages 160-161, wyrozemski2023transcriptomicandfunctional pages 71-75).

Together, these 3D organoid and co-culture platforms provide a sophisticated framework that recapitulates the in vivo milieu—incorporating epithelial, mesenchymal, and immune components—to investigate the antigen processing and presentation of deamidated gliadin peptides. The comprehensive functional readouts, spanning cytokine profiles, T-cell proliferation, activation markers, apoptosis, and transcriptional analysis, yield robust mechanistic insights into TCR activation and downstream immune cascades in celiac disease (santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 1-2, santos2024ahumanautoimmune pages 34-37).
